## Description

Editorial audit on 2026-05-03 surfaced a **coverage hole**: the **Management** section is referenced as a first-class platform UI navigation surface in multiple places but has **no dedicated documentation** explaining what it is or what it contains.

### References to the Management section across the doc tree

| File | Line | Context |
|---|---|---|
| Features.md | 90 | "search and filtering are available not only in the Catalog tab, but in Query examples, Master Data, **Management** and Dictionary tabs as well" |
| Features.md | 698 | "**DELETED** status highlights that a specific entity is going to be deleted from the **Management page**" |
| Features.md | 733 | "**Integration Wizard** is an in-app UI under **Management → Integrations**" |
| integrations/integration-wizard.md | 5, 26 | "in-app UI under Management → Integrations"; "Open Management → Integrations in the platform UI" |
| configuration-and-deployment/trylocally.md | 31, 42, 58 | URL `http://localhost:8080/management/datasources` (used for verification); `http://localhost:8080/management/collectors` for adding a collector |
| configuration-and-deployment/enable-security/authorization/owners.md | 5 | "manage owners in `Management` section under `Owners` tab" |
| configuration-and-deployment/enable-security/authorization/user-owner-association.md | 20 | "In `Management` section under `Associations` tab" |
| integrations/README.md | 114 | "Management → Collectors" (token issuance) |
| many integration pages | various | "Management → Collectors" referenced for token creation |

### What's missing

- **No page describes what the Management section is** — its conceptual scope, what tabs it contains, who is meant to use it.
- The Management section appears to host: Datasources, Collectors, Integrations, Owners, Associations, Roles, Policies, Tags, Namespaces (deduced from cross-references). But this is not documented anywhere as a single coherent surface.
- The Management section is not in `SUMMARY.md` as a feature page (no Management entry in the top-level navigation).
- `Features.md` has feature pages for many things that LIVE in the Management section (Lookup Tables, Integration Wizards, Custom navigation links via `odd.links`, Owner section, etc.) but does not have a "Management" feature description.

### Why this is a coherence problem

A reader landing on `Features.md` line 90 sees "Management" listed alongside Catalog, Query examples, Master Data, Dictionary — implying it's a first-class platform area on par with those — but cannot find what the Management section actually does. They'd have to visit the platform UI to discover its contents. The doc product names a primary navigation surface and provides no description of it.

A new operator reading the docs end-to-end will form a mental model where `Management` is a meaningful word but its referent is opaque. Three years from now, "what is the Management section?" is the kind of question whose answer should be a single landing page; today the answer is "navigate from the cross-references and infer".

## Failure shape

**Coverage hole** + **cross-audience absence** (the Management section appears in operator + developer + user surfaces but is not documented for any of them) (per `playbooks/doc-product-editorial-read.md` failure-shape table).

## Context

- **Why medium (not high)**: the doc product is internally consistent in NOT documenting Management, and operators figure it out from the UI. But per the world-class doc mission, "a complete manual" implies coverage of every primary navigation surface — Management is the operator-facing counterpart of Catalog (the user-facing surface), and both deserve equivalent treatment.
- **Why not low**: the cross-references are pervasive — 8+ pages mention Management as the place to go, but a reader who doesn't know what Management is can't follow the trail.
- **Recurring pattern**: every named UI surface that appears in 3+ cross-references should have a documentation home. Worth a Gate-7 extension: when a term is mentioned ≥3 times across the doc tree without a dedicated section, surface as a coverage-hole finding.
