## Description

Editorial audit on `/review batch:feature/docs-editorial-followups-2026-05-06` surfaced an **A8 vocabulary-consistency** defect introduced by DOC-136's freshly-shipped Features.md disclaimer.

### The conflation

`docs/Features.md:3` (DOC-136 disclaimer):

> each feature below points at (a) its **per-pillar canonical home** ([Data Discovery](data-discovery.md), [Data Modelling](data-modelling.md), [Master Data Management](master-data-management.md), [Management](management.md)), …

The disclaimer labels four destinations as "per-pillar canonical homes". But the doc-product's canonical taxonomy distinguishes:

- **Three governance pillars** — read-oriented, conceptual, all in the Data Governance map: Data Discovery, Data Modelling, Master Data Management.
- **Management** — the operator-mutating UI surface (per `management.md:7`: "Where the Catalog and the Directory are read-oriented (a user finds an existing entity), Management is mutating"). Not a governance pillar; not in the Data Governance map.

The cross-page coherence chain confirms the canonical framing:

| Source | Verbatim |
|---|---|
| `data-discovery.md:24` | "Data Discovery, Data Modelling, and Master Data Management are all top-level **governance pillars**" |
| `data-discovery.md:30` | "**The three pillars** sit alongside each other in the Data Governance map, not nested." |
| `data-discovery.md:38` | "the position of Data Discovery among the **other governance pillars** → [Main Concepts → Data Governance map]" |
| `master-data-management.md:29` | "the position of Master Data Management among the **other governance pillars** (Data Discovery, Data Lineage, Data Quality, Data Modelling, Data Glossary)" |
| `main-concepts.md:43-52` | "Data Governance map" enumerates 8 pillars; **Management is not on the list**. |
| `Features.md:3` (new — DOC-136) | "**per-pillar canonical home** ([Data Discovery], [Data Modelling], [Master Data Management], [Management])" — Management labeled a pillar alongside the three governance pillars |

### Why this slipped past DOC-136's review

The 10 Quality Bar gates verify the per-item commit's correctness. The disclaimer is structurally sound (right page, right placement, right cross-link targets, right tone) — it passes every per-item gate. The vocabulary conflation is an A8 (vocabulary consistency) editorial defect that surfaces when the disclaimer is read in cross-page context against `data-discovery.md`, `master-data-management.md`, and `main-concepts.md`. Per `playbooks/doc-product-editorial-read.md`, this is exactly the failure shape the editorial audit is designed to catch (an item ships locally correctly while introducing global vocabulary drift).

### Reader-experience cost

A reader who lands on Features.md and clicks the disclaimer's link to Management (expecting "another governance pillar") arrives at a page that frames itself as "the operator-facing surface inside the platform UI: every tab here lets an operator add, edit, or remove a piece of catalog configuration." The reader notices the framing mismatch — Management is doing something different from what the disclaimer promised. Trust in the disclaimer (and by extension the doc product) drops slightly.

A reader who reads the Data Governance map on `main-concepts.md` and counts pillars (Data Discovery, Data Lineage, Data Quality, Data Modelling, Data Glossary, Master Data Management, Data Cost, Data Security) lands on Features.md and hears the disclaimer call Management a fourth pillar. The two surfaces send incompatible vocabulary signals.

## Failure shape

**A8 (Vocabulary consistency) — canonical-term over-extension** (per `pillars/documentation/quality-framework.md` A8 audit cue). The term "pillar" is canonical for the governance-pillar surfaces in `data-discovery.md` / `master-data-management.md` / `main-concepts.md`. DOC-136's disclaimer over-extends it to include Management, which the canonical taxonomy frames as a distinct (operator-mutating) concept.

This is a sub-shape of A8 worth cataloging: **canonical-term over-extension** — using a precise canonical term in a broader sense that conflates two distinct concepts. Adjacent to "parallel surfaces with drift" (DOC-135's failure shape) but distinct: there's no parallel surface here — there's a single new surface using a canonical term in a non-canonical way.

## Context

- **Why low**: cosmetic vocabulary defect; readers can still navigate, the disclaimer's intent (link each Features.md section to its top-level home) remains correct. But the world-class doc bar holds that canonical terms should be used canonically — every reader landing through any entry surface should encounter the same vocabulary.
- **Why log at all**: per A8 (vocabulary consistency from `pillars/documentation/quality-framework.md`), the term "pillar" is reserved for the three governance pillars. Over-extending it to Management drifts the canonical taxonomy. Editorial-audit case-law: DOC-099 (Phase A) closed an analogous category-vs-implementation conflation on the Collector term. This item closes the analogous "pillar"-term conflation introduced by DOC-136.
- **Why the per-item gates didn't catch it**: DOC-136's 10 gates verify the disclaimer is structurally sound (right page, right placement, right cross-link targets, right tone). The cross-page vocabulary check is the editorial audit's domain (A8), not a per-item gate.
- **Recurring pattern**: every disclaimer / orientation paragraph that names multiple top-level surfaces should disambiguate **kind** (governance pillar vs. operator surface vs. developer guide etc.). Worth folding into the editorial-audit cue list as an A8 sub-shape: "canonical-term over-extension on orientation prose".
